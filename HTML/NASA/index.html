Solar System

Piece 1 of the Universe — our cosmic neighborhood

Overview: The Solar System consists of the Sun, eight major planets, moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and more.

  • Central star: The Sun
  • Planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
  • Dwarf planets: Pluto, Eris, Haumea, etc.
  • Moons: Over 200 known
  • Other: Asteroid Belt, Kuiper Belt, Oort Cloud
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Earth

Piece 2 of the Universe — the world we call home

Overview: Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known place in the universe confirmed to support life.

  • Radius: ~6,371 km
  • Mass: ~5.97 × 1024 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~1 AU
  • Surface: ~71% water, 29% land
  • Atmosphere: Nitrogen (78%), Oxygen (21%)
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Mercury

Piece 3 of the Universe — the smallest planet in our Solar System

Overview: Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun, a small, rocky world with extreme temperature variations and a thin atmosphere.

  • Radius: ~2,439 km
  • Mass: ~3.30 × 1023 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~0.39 AU
  • Surface: Rocky, cratered
  • Atmosphere: Very thin, mostly oxygen, sodium, hydrogen
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Venus

Piece 4 of the Universe — Earth's twin in size and structure

Overview: Venus is the second planet from the Sun, known for its thick, toxic atmosphere and scorching surface temperatures.

  • Radius: ~6,052 km
  • Mass: ~4.87 × 1024 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~0.72 AU
  • Surface: Rocky, volcanic
  • Atmosphere: Thick, mainly carbon dioxide, clouds of sulfuric acid
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Mars

Piece 5 of the Universe — the Red Planet

Overview: Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, known for its reddish appearance due to iron oxide on its surface.

  • Radius: ~3,390 km
  • Mass: ~6.42 × 1023 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~1.52 AU
  • Surface: Rocky, deserts, polar ice caps
  • Atmosphere: Thin, mostly CO₂, some nitrogen and argon
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Jupiter

Piece 6 of the Universe — the gas giant

Overview: Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System, a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot and many moons.

  • Radius: ~69,911 km
  • Mass: ~1.90 × 1027 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~5.20 AU
  • Surface: Gaseous, no solid surface
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen and helium, clouds, storms
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Saturn

Piece 7 of the Universe — the ringed planet

Overview: Saturn is famous for its extensive ring system and is the second-largest planet in the Solar System.

  • Radius: ~58,232 km
  • Mass: ~5.68 × 1026 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~9.58 AU
  • Surface: Gaseous, no solid surface
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen and helium, with cloud layers
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Uranus

Piece 8 of the Universe — the tilted ice giant

Overview: Uranus is an ice giant with a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere and rotates on its side.

  • Radius: ~25,362 km
  • Mass: ~8.68 × 1025 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~19.2 AU
  • Surface: Gaseous, icy composition
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, methane
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Neptune

Piece 9 of the Universe — the farthest known planet

Overview: Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun, an ice giant with strong winds and storms, known for its deep blue color.

  • Radius: ~24,622 km
  • Mass: ~1.02 × 1026 kg
  • Distance from Sun: ~30.1 AU
  • Surface: Gaseous, icy composition
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, methane
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Dark Matter

Piece 10 of the Universe — mysterious invisible matter

Overview: Dark matter is a type of matter that does not emit, absorb or reflect light, making it invisible, but it has gravitational effects on galaxies and the universe.

  • Composition: Unknown, detected by gravity
  • Influence: Galaxy rotation, cosmic structure
  • Discovery: 1930s, Fritz Zwicky
Image credit: Wikipedia / public domain.

Cosmology

Piece 11 of the Universe — the study of the universe

Overview: Cosmology is the scientific study of the origin, evolution, and eventual fate of the universe.

  • Topics: Big Bang, dark energy, dark matter
  • Methods: Observations, simulations, theory
  • Goal: Understand universe structure & evolution
Image credit: Wikipedia / public domain.

Satellite

Piece 12 of the Universe — artificial and natural companions in space

Overview: A satellite is an object that orbits a planet or star. Satellites can be natural, like the Moon, or artificial, like the Hubble Space Telescope and GPS satellites.

  • Types: Natural (e.g., Moon), Artificial (e.g., ISS)
  • Uses: Communication, navigation, weather, research
  • First Artificial Satellite: Sputnik 1 (1957)
  • Orbit Types: Low Earth, Geostationary, Polar
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Sun

Piece of the Universe — the star at the center of our Solar System

Overview: The Sun is a giant ball of hot plasma and the central star of our Solar System, providing the energy that sustains life on Earth.

  • Type: G-type main-sequence star (G2V)
  • Radius: ~696,340 km
  • Mass: ~1.989 × 1030 kg
  • Age: ~4.6 billion years
  • Energy Source: Nuclear fusion of hydrogen
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Moon

Piece of the Universe — Earth’s only natural satellite

Overview: The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite, influencing tides and stabilizing Earth’s rotation.

  • Radius: ~1,737 km
  • Distance from Earth: ~384,400 km
  • Surface: Rocky, cratered
  • Gravity: ~1/6 of Earth’s
  • Exploration: First human landing in 1969 (Apollo 11)
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Meteoroids

Piece of the Universe — small rocky or metallic bodies in space

Overview: Meteoroids are small chunks of rock or metal from asteroids or comets. When they enter Earth’s atmosphere, they become meteors or shooting stars.

  • Size: Dust grains to 1 meter
  • Source: Comets, asteroids, planetary collisions
  • In Atmosphere: Called meteors
  • On Ground: Called meteorites
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Dwarf Planets

Piece of the Universe — small planetary bodies

Overview: Dwarf planets are celestial bodies that orbit the Sun, are spherical, but have not cleared their orbital path of other objects. Pluto is the most famous example.

  • Examples: Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, Ceres
  • Orbit: Mostly in the Kuiper Belt
  • Characteristics: Smaller than planets, icy/rocky
Image credit: NASA / public domain.

Comets

Piece of the Universe — icy bodies with glowing tails

Overview: Comets are icy objects from the outer solar system. When near the Sun, heat causes their ice to vaporize, forming a bright coma and a glowing tail.

  • Composition: Ice, dust, rock
  • Orbit: Highly elliptical
  • Famous Examples: Halley’s Comet, Hale–Bopp
  • Tail: Always points away from the Sun
Image credit: ESA / NASA.

Asteroids

Piece of the Universe — rocky bodies orbiting the Sun

Overview: Asteroids are rocky remnants from the early solar system, mostly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.

  • Location: Mainly asteroid belt
  • Composition: Rock, metal, sometimes ice
  • Size: Few meters to hundreds of kilometers
  • Famous Examples: Ceres, Vesta, Eros
Image credit: NASA / public domain.
